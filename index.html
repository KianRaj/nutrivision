Ingredient-aware hierarchical frequency fusion for single-image food nutrition estimation

— a multimodal study on Nutrition5k and OmniFood8K —

Abstract

Photo-based dietary assessment requires turning a single RGB image of a plated meal into five physically meaningful numbers — calories, mass, fat, carbohydrate, and protein. Prior systems lean on real depth sensors, multi-view rigs, or manually-curated ingredient annotations that are unavailable at deployment time. We propose NutriVision, a single-image nutrition estimator that combines a dual ConvNeXt-Base backbone, an ingredient-conditioned frequency-domain fusion module (IC-FAFM), and an ingredient-aware multi-prediction head (IA-MPH). Depth is estimated from RGB by DepthAnything V3; ingredient text is encoded by a frozen CLIP ViT-B/32; the food region is isolated by a SAM3 mask. Across two challenging benchmarks our model reaches 13.48 % mean PMAE on Nutrition5k and 10.89 % on OmniFood8K — improving over the best published depth-using baselines on both datasets, while using only RGB and an optional ingredient list at inference.

Why this is hard

Two pieces of information are needed: what is on the plate (semantic identification of each ingredient) and how much of it (a 3-D volume, scaled by density to a mass). From a single 2-D photo, neither is directly observable. Existing approaches either rely on fiducial markers or known-depth rigs (limiting deployment), or treat the problem as a flat regression from RGB pixels (losing volumetric grounding). NutriVision threads the needle by re-introducing geometry through estimated depth and re-introducing semantics through ingredient text, then fusing them in a way that respects the structure of each signal.

Contributions

  1. IC-FAFM (Ingredient-Conditioned Frequency-Aligned Fusion Module) — a 2-D FFT fusion stage that lets depth dominate the low-frequency band (shape, volume) and RGB dominate the high-frequency band (texture, fine detail), with the mix conditioned by a CLIP-encoded ingredient string.
  2. IA-MPH (Ingredient-Aware Multi-Prediction Head) — an ingredient-gated channel selection block followed by 1×1 cross-attention, jointly emitting all five nutrient predictions from a single shared trunk.
  3. Two-dataset validation — reaching 13.48 % mean PMAE on Nutrition5k (Google CVPR'21) and 10.89 % on OmniFood8K (CVPR'26), both with a single RGB input and DA3-estimated depth.
  4. Honest ablations on SSRA — the Scale-Shift Residual Adapter, retained for theoretical consistency with prior multi-modal nutrition systems, contributes at the noise floor (Δ ≤ 0.1 % mean PMAE) on Nutrition5k's fixed-rig cameras; the depth-loader fix (v1 uint8 → v2 raw float) accounts for most of the recovered scale signal.
Method

Architecture

RGB and DA3 depth pass through a dual ConvNeXt-Base + ISM stack; CLIP-encoded ingredient text conditions both the frequency-domain fusion (IC-FAFM) and the prediction head (IA-MPH).

1

SSRA

Refines DA3 monocular depth via global scale-shift + a local CNN residual.

2

Dual ConvNeXt-B + ISM

Two ImageNet-22k pretrained backbones with window-attention refinement at 4 scales.

3

IC-FAFM

FFT split + ingredient cross-attention fuses RGB and depth at every scale.

4

IA-MPH

Ingredient-gated channel selection + cross-attention head predicts 5 nutrients.

Pipeline How one RGB photo becomes five nutrient numbers

Swipe right (or use the arrows) to follow the same plate through every stage — from the raw photo, through depth + mask precompute, into two backbones, into the FFT fusion that mixes them, and out as five numbers.

Step 1

Input RGB photo

A single 384 × 384 plated-meal image. No multi-view rig, no depth sensor — just one phone-quality picture.

tensor → 3 × 384 × 384
Step 2

+ Monocular depth (DA3)

DepthAnything V3 turns the photo into a relative depth map. Cold colours = far, warm = near. This is the only place 3-D shape information enters the network.

tensor → 1 × 384 × 384 (raw float)
Step 3

+ Food mask (SAM3)

SAM3 produces a binary food-vs-background mask, so neither backbone wastes capacity learning about tablecloth, shadow, or cutlery — only the food survives.

tensor → 1 × 384 × 384 ∈ {0, 1}
Step 4
D̃ = α · DDA3 + β + CNNres(DDA3)
scale + shift + small residual

SSRA rescaling

DA3 only knows depth up to a scale. The Scale-Shift Residual Adapter learns a per-image affine plus a small CNN correction — nudging it toward the metric depth a calibrated sensor would give.

depth becomes scale-aware
Step 5
96²48²24²12²
RGB branch — ConvNeXt-B + ISM

RGB features (appearance)

ConvNeXt-B + ISM builds a 4-scale RGB pyramid. This branch catches colour, sauce gloss, char, grain texture, leaf veins — the cues that tell calories per gram.

Frgb → 4 scales · 128 → 1024 ch
Step 6
96²48²24²12²
Depth branch — ConvNeXt-B + ISM

Depth features (volume)

A second, parallel ConvNeXt-B runs on the rescaled depth and builds its own 4-scale pyramid. This branch catches thickness, mound height, occlusion order — the cues that tell grams.

Fdep → 4 scales · 128 → 1024 ch
Step 7
Frgb→ FFT →lowhigh
Fdep→ FFT →lowhigh
↓ mix · CLIP(ingredients) as key ↓
IFFT → Ffused

IC-FAFM frequency fusion

At every scale we 2-D FFT both feature maps. Depth dominates the low band (shape, volume); RGB dominates the high band (texture). The CLIP ingredient embedding conditions the mix so "salmon, rice" emphasises protein-relevant bands differently from "salad, oil".

Ffused · ingredient-aware · 4 scales

IA-MPH head

Five numbers

The Ingredient-Aware Multi-Prediction Head pools the four fused scales, applies an ingredient-gated channel-selection block, then five small linear heads emit the final predictions. Trained jointly with per-nutrient MAE loss.
